# NexaFlow Financial Output Policy

Use this policy whenever a NexaFlow skill produces analysis, report text, or evidence review.

- Separate facts, inferences, hypotheses, and missing data.
- State what data was read before making conclusions: tables, fields, periods, external evidence, knowledge, and memory.
- Treat Jira, DingTalk, Confluence, and documents as project/document evidence, not proof of release or business impact.
- Treat external evidence without event time as weak context. Show source URL, publish time, collected time, and limitation.
- Do not provide investment advice, return promises, compliance evasion, or instructions to bypass risk controls.
- Keep financial product recommendations operational: product flow, campaign, channel, funnel, UX, risk policy, monitoring, and data requests.
- Format all percentages and percentage-point values to two decimal places.
- If evidence conflicts with accepted correction memory, use the correction and mark the old conclusion as superseded.
